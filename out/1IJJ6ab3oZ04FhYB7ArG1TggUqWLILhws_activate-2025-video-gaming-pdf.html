TL;DR

Gaming will surpass 3.5B adult players by 2028, with growth accelerating after 2024. Global consumer game revenue is projected at roughly $217B in 2028, with mobile leading growth. Mobile already contributes over half of revenue and re-accelerates through 2028. Established franchises dominate top paid PC/console charts with few new IPs breaking in.

5 Insights

  1. Active gamers are expected to exceed 3.5B by 2028 (≈3,591M), with global gamer growth at ~6% CAGR (2019–2024E) accelerating to ~7% (2024E–2028E).
  2. Global consumer video game revenue is projected to reach ≈$217B in 2028E (from ≈$169B in 2024E), implying ~6% 2024E–2028E CAGR; 2028E split ≈$61B U.S. and ≈$157B Rest of World (figures may not sum due to rounding).
  3. By platform, mobile grows from ≈$66B (2019) to ≈$124B (2028E), outpacing console (≈$51B) and PC (≈$43B); 2024E–2028E CAGRs are ~8% (mobile), ~3% (console), and ~4% (PC).
  4. Mobile accounts for over half of consumer game revenue in 2024E (≈$90B of ≈$169B) and shifts from ~1% CAGR (2021–2024E) to ~8% CAGR (2024E–2028E), indicating renewed momentum.
  5. Top-earning paid PC/console titles (U.S., 2021–2023) are predominantly sequels from existing franchises, with very few new franchises entering the annual top 10 (exact counts unclear in extract, but the report explicitly highlights this pattern).

Commentary

For brands and performance marketers, the center of gravity is mobile: it already represents the majority of game revenue and is set to grow fastest through 2028, making it the most scalable channel for incremental reach and ROAS. Creative and measurement should be mobile-first (e.g., optimizing for in‑app events and LTV), while console/PC can be leveraged for high‑impact integrations tied to established franchises—where purchase intent clusters. Expect regional revenue expansion mainly outside the U.S., underscoring the need for localized creatives and privacy‑resilient attribution. Given franchise dominance, IP partnerships and sequel‑aligned launches can boost conversion efficiency and predictability in media mix models.
