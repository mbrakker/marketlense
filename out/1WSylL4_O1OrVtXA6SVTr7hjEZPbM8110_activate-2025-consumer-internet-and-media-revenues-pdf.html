TL;DR

Activate forecasts global consumer internet and media revenues to grow by about $403B from 2024E to 2028E, at a 3.7% CAGR. Advertising is expected to drive more than half of that growth. Subscriptions will account for the vast majority of consumer spend growth. Overall spend levels approach roughly $3.0T by 2028E.

5 Insights

  1. Total global consumer internet and media revenues are projected to add approximately $403B between 2024E and 2028E, reaching about $3.0T in 2028E (Activate forecast) with a 3.7% CAGR.
  2. Advertising is the primary growth engine, contributing over half of total growth and growing at a 5.9% CAGR from 2024E to 2028E.
  3. Paid content is forecast to grow at a 2.1% CAGR and the cost of internet access at 2.7% CAGR (2024E–2028E), both trailing the overall industry CAGR of 3.7%.
  4. Subscriptions will drive most consumer spend growth, adding $136B by 2028E versus $40B from single transactions.
  5. Consumer spend on internet and media grows at roughly a 2.5% CAGR; based on chart labels, subscription revenue grows around 3.3% CAGR while single transactions grow around 2.2% CAGR (minor uncertainty due to label placement).

Commentary

For brands, the outsized role of advertising in industry growth implies that performance and brand budgets should increasingly favor ad-supported environments with robust reach and targeting. To protect and improve ROAS, marketers should double down on incrementality testing, MMM + MTA triangulation, and creative/format optimization within high-growth channels. The subscription-led rise in consumer spend suggests persistent pay willingness, but ad growth indicates scalable demand-gen opportunities; balancing acquisition (CAC/LTV discipline) with upper-funnel reach will be key. Measurement frameworks must accommodate mixed business models (ad- and sub-supported) and capture cross-channel lift to allocate to the fastest-growing segments reliably.
